The Hidden Cost of the Second-Cheapest Bottle

It's the most predictable order in hospitality — and one of the most expensive habits a restaurant can leave unaddressed.

8 April 2026·5 min read

Watch a table order wine without guidance and you can almost predict the choice. Not the cheapest bottle — that feels stingy — but the one just above it. The second-cheapest. Every time.

Why the second-cheapest wins

It's pure psychology. The cheapest option signals frugality, so diners instinctively avoid it. The next one up feels like a safe, respectable compromise — sensible without being cheap. Restaurants have understood this for decades, which is why the second-cheapest bottle is so often the one carrying the heaviest markup.

The cost to the guest

The guest pays twice. They frequently overpay relative to the actual quality, and — more importantly — they miss the wine that would have made their meal. A default chosen out of social caution is rarely the bottle that pairs best with what they're eating.

The cost to the restaurant

It's tempting to see a guaranteed mid-margin sale as a win. It isn't, for three reasons:

  • Lower total spend. A guided guest who finds the right pairing often trades up — and orders a second bottle they enjoyed.
  • A flatter experience. A forgettable wine makes a forgettable meal, and forgettable meals don't generate return visits or recommendations.
  • A stagnant list. When everyone defaults to the same two bottles, the rest of your carefully chosen list never moves.
Without guidance, most diners default to the second-cheapest bottle. The right recommendation changes that — and changes the bill.

Replacing the default with a recommendation

The fix isn't a hard upsell — it's guidance. When a guest is shown a wine that genuinely suits their dish, the decision stops being about price signalling and starts being about the meal. Spend rises because satisfaction rises.

This is what Entwine puts at every table: a pairing matched to the specific dish a guest is ordering, drawn from the restaurant's real list. The second-cheapest bottle stops being the path of least resistance, because there's now a better, clearer choice in front of every guest.
